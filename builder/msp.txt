MSP
===
Work is being done on supporting MSP (Mud Sound Protocol) on the Mudlet platform
for the MUD.  This document will outline the current state of implementation as
well as future plans.

CURRENTLY IMPLEMENTED
=====================

SCONFIG
=======

The player command 'sconfig' (see HELP SCONFIG) is used by players to customise
their individual sound environment.  It enables turning sound on/off completely
and by sound category, and volume scaling on a "master" and per-category level.

Ambient and music sound
=======================

Ambient sound and music are separate looping media layers. They can play at the
same time and are controlled independently through the player's sound config.

Ambient sound
=============

All sector types (forest, hills, etc.) have ambient loops associated with them.
These can be overridden on a per-area or per-room basis.

Ambient sound uses the following priority:

room ambient -> area ambient -> sector ambient

To put an ambient loop on a whole area, use the following syntax in the
#AREA_SPECIAL section:

#AREA_SPECIAL
ambient ambient/area/abyss.mp3
ambient_vol 30

The above tells the MUD to load abyss.mp3 as the default environmental loop for
the area, using a path relative to the "audio" directory, at a source volume of
30/100.

Music
=====

Music is a separate layer from ambient sound. Area music plays throughout an
area unless it is overridden by music assigned to a specific room.

Music uses the following priority:

room music -> area music -> no music

To put a music loop on a whole area, use the following syntax in the
#AREA_SPECIAL section:

#AREA_SPECIAL
music music/area/abyss.mp3
music_vol 20

The above tells the MUD to load abyss.mp3 as the default looping music for the
area, using a path relative to the "audio" directory, at a source volume of
20/100.

An area may define both ambient sound and music:

#AREA_SPECIAL
ambient ambient/area/abyss.mp3
ambient_vol 30
music music/area/abyss.mp3
music_vol 20

Room overrides
==============

Room-specific ambient and music loops are defined in the #ROOMS_AMBIENT
section. This section must appear after the area's #ROOMS section.

The media layer is determined by the beginning of the file path:

ambient/    Environmental ambient layer
music/      Music layer

Example:

#ROOMS_AMBIENT
9320 ambient/room/9320.mp3 80
9320 music/room/9320.mp3 30
$

The above assigns both a room ambient loop and a room music loop to room 9320.

A room may have only one of the two layers defined:

#ROOMS_AMBIENT
9320 music/room/9320.mp3 30
9321 ambient/room/9321.mp3 80
$

Room ambient overrides area and sector ambient. Room music overrides area
music. Leaving a room with a music override causes the area's music to resume.

Volumes
=======

The volume following each filename is the source volume from 0 to 100.

0       Disables that definition.
1..100  Valid source volume.

The final volume heard by a player is also affected by their master, ambient,
or music volume settings.

Directory structure
===================

The currently implemented sound directory structure under the "gui/custom"
directory is (additional subdirectories exist for some):

Weather
=======

Weather effects are automatically layered over ambient sound. They currently
exist for rain and lightning.

Weather effects belong to the ambient/environment layer and do not replace or
interrupt the separate music layer.

SFX
===

Currently some SFX exist. Mostly for opening/closing/locking/unlocking doors and
basic item consumption sounds.

Door sounds can be overridden with custom sounds like so:

#EXITS_SFX
10043 s open sfx/door/open_cupboard1.mp3 75
10043 s close sfx/door/close_cupboard1.mp3 70
10082 n open sfx/door/open_cupboard1.mp3 75
10082 n close sfx/door/close_cupboard1.mp3 70
$

Consumption sounds are in sfx->misc but cannot currently be overridden, only
turned off (along with all SFX) with SCONFIG.

Notifications
=============

There are notification sounds for when different comms channels are used, when
auctions start, and when characters level.  These cannot currently be
customised, only turned on/off or have their relative volume adjusted.

Overridable Footstep Foley for NPCs
===================================

By default mobiles have the footstep sounds of a medium-sized humanoid, modified
by terrain type.  If you wish to override these on a per-mob basis, you can do
it like so:

Add any of the following keys (for the terrain-type foley you want to override
for the mob) after the mob's #MOBILES entry, where the "3060" is the mob's vnum:

FStepInside sfx.foley.mob.3060.inside~
FStepCity sfx.foley.mob.3060.city~
FStepField sfx.foley.mob.3060.field~
FStepForest sfx.foley.mob.3060.forest~
FStepHills sfx.foley.mob.3060.hills~
FStepMountain sfx.foley.mob.3060.mountain~
FStepDesert sfx.foley.mob.3060.desert~
FStepSwamp sfx.foley.mob.3060.swamp~
FStepWater sfx.foley.mob.3060.water~
FStepUnderwater sfx.foley.mob.3060.underwater~
FStepUnderwaterGround sfx.foley.mob.3060.underwater_ground~
FStepAir sfx.foley.mob.3060.air~

Example:

cityguard guard~
the cityguard~
A cityguard stands here.
~
A big, strong, helpful, trustworthy guard.
~
1|64|32768|16777216 0 1000 S
15 15 -2 15d15+150 1d4+10
0 0
8 8 1
FStepCity sfx.foley.mob.3060.city~
FStepInside sfx.foley.mob.3060.inside~


Then add entries to the sound_const.c sound registry file, below will randomly
select from a few files for either terrain type:

{ "sfx.foley.mob.3060.city",
{ "sfx/foley/mob/3060/city.1.mp3", "sfx/foley/mob/3060/city.2.mp3", NULL },
{ 0 }, 45, "foley", 1 },
{ "sfx.foley.mob.3060.inside",
{ "sfx/foley/mob/3060/inside.1.mp3", "sfx/foley/mob/3060/inside.2.mp3", NULL },
{ 0 }, 45, "foley", 1 },

Now just recompile, ensure the sound files are uploaded to the relevant folders
on the MUD server (currently under appropriate sub-directories of
/var/www/smihilist.com/dd4/web/main/gui/custom/audio/) and BOOM.
